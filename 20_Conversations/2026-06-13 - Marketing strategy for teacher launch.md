# 2026-06-13 — Marketing strategy for teacher launch

## Context

Samuel asked, as a marketing question, how to market the Opener to teachers as launch approaches: social media vs word of mouth. Framing/strategy talk, no hard decisions locked.

## Key points

- Reframed the binary: word of mouth is the OUTCOME you engineer, not a channel you run. In EdTech, peer-teacher recommendation is the highest-converting path. "Social media" for a ~10k-teacher niche should mean community presence, not paid broadcast ads.
- Recommended motion = product-led + community-led growth that manufactures word of mouth. Skip paid social through year one (wrong economics for a niche pre-launch product on a tight budget).
- Priority order recommended:
  1. Founder-led seeding of a small, obsessed first cohort (20-50 who love it), drawn from the warm network + Airtable CRM. Doubles as The Garage evidence.
  2. Product as marketing: a free week of real Openers, no card, is the campaign.
  3. Built-in viral mechanic: student copy handed to 30 kids + shown in a room; add a "forward to a colleague" footer link and tasteful attribution on the student copy.
  4. Community presence: AP English Language Teachers FB group, College Board AP Community, #APLang, NCTE, Teachers Pay Teachers (discovery funnel). Show up as a contributor, not a vendor.
  5. No paid ads.
- TIMING overrides everything: teachers lock daily routines in the first two weeks of the year, so a daily lesson-opener must claim the bell-ringer slot BEFORE the year starts. Front-load marketing to July-August. AP Summer Institutes (APSIs) are the highest-density room of exact buyers right before back-to-school.
- Cautions: don't build an unsustainable solo content calendar; don't let marketing outrun product readiness (human blind-test panel unrun, pre-guardrail partisan samples to pull, Stripe unbuilt) — a churned early user is a lost advocate plus a detractor.
