# 2026-05-22 — Article Source Material

## Context

Samuel asked whether AP Lang's canon of famous/commencement speeches (different kinds of prose than current nonfiction) should be incorporated into Caught Up AI's source material. Also asked, out of curiosity, about accessing Claude from his phone.

## Key points

- AP Lang canon (JFK, MLK, Wallace's Kenyon address, Saunders, FDR, etc.) is genuinely on the exam and taught, but teachers have no prep pain with it: public domain, already owned, spoon-fed by curriculum guides.
- Baking canon into the default cadence would dilute the "current within 7 days" value prop and drift toward a different product (canonical text library) with its own sourcing/attribution work.
- Phone access: it is not a plugin. Two official features — Dispatch (phone fires tasks at a Cowork agent on the desktop, built on Computer Use) and Remote Control (continue a local Claude Code session from the phone via QR code). Remote Control fits the vault/Code workflow; Dispatch fits long agent jobs. Both require the desktop to stay awake/online. Samuel was just curious, no setup requested.

## Decisions

- D-004: Keep the canon OUT of the default content cadence for the September pilot. Reaffirms locked decision: current nonfiction, open/CC/PD, 350-450 words. Canon becomes a post-MVP expansion lever ("Speech of the Week") and/or a discovery probe — not a launch feature.
