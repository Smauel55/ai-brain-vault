# 2026-06-07 - Sample Openers were evergreen by design

## Question
- Samuel wanted a ready answer to a teacher-facing question: the product claims ~7-day-current topics, but several sample Openers (the penny, the public library, the starling murmuration) were not about events. Were those pulled from a recent article or unrelated?

## Answer (verified against source files)
- Those three were **illustrative samples, not products of the real sourcing pipeline.** Not pulled from any 7-day window.
- They were chosen for register variety AND **accuracy-safety** (evergreen, stable-fact topics) so the demo couldn't be wrong on a fact. The render script header says so directly: "Registers chosen for variety and accuracy-safety" ([[render_samples_batch.py]] in Sample-Briefs).
- Penny = Ledger, Free to All / library = Tribute, No Bird in Charge / starlings = Long Look.

## How to answer a teacher
- Those specific pieces: no, evergreen-by-design samples showing format + voice range.
- The real product is meant to run off current sources, BUT the cadence/currency claim is still being pressure-tested. Per [[Content-Sourcing]]: do not advertise a cadence until a 2-4 week real pull counts survivors.
- Do NOT claim the three samples were current pieces.

## Positioning point (Samuel's instinct was right)
- Evergreen / non-event prose is legitimate AP Lang material; the strongest exam source passages are often timeless essays, not breaking news. A mix of current + durable-topic pieces is closer to the real AP corpus, not a weakness.
- "7 days concurrent" is really about freshness/relevance, not a rule that every piece hooks to an event.
