- WHAT: On 2026-06-04, after three AI essays were written to a strict writing manual (open/build/close spine, one structural engine, long-vs-short rhythm, terse one-line button close), Samuel rejected the method: "It's too obvious that you are following a set of rules. Every essay has the same start, end, and cadence."
- WHY: A tight spec produces uniform output by design. Across multiple pieces the shared skeleton itself becomes the tell, which is fatal when the goal is to not look generated (the blind teacher test; AP Lang teachers who read for craft).
- HOW TO APPLY: When generating more than one AI piece, do NOT reuse a single structural template. Vary the opening type (not always a 2-3 sentence punch), the closing type (not always a terse button), the structural engine, and the overall cadence across pieces. A manual should set quality floors and bans, not one fixed shape. Treat cross-piece sameness as its own AI tell, alongside per-piece tells.
- BROADER IMPLICATION (project): rule-driven AI prose drifts to a detectable house style; this is a real cost of the AI-with-editing content engine and a point toward commissioned/real authored content. See 10_Projects/Caught Up AI/Content-Sourcing.md.
