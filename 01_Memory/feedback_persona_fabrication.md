For Caught Up AI's full AI-generated openers, it is OK to fabricate the writer as a regular person, and to invent friends and family, to serve the article's purpose. A constructed first-person persona is acceptable, not a dishonesty problem.

The line is HARD FACTS only: statistics, named figures, dated events, history, science, anything checkable must be true and sourced (see [[Accuracy-Guardrail]]). Invented personal experience and relationships are not "facts" in this sense and are fair game.

**Why:** Samuel said so on 2026-06-06 when I had deferred the Witness Stand and Open Letter registers out of concern that first-person memoir meant fabricating real experience. He does not see persona invention as a concern; only fabricated hard facts are.

**How to apply:** The first-person registers ([[Register-Specs]] Witness Stand, Open Letter) are usable. Write the "I" and any people in the piece freely to fit the rhetorical goal, but never invent or bend a statistic, a historical event, or any checkable claim.
