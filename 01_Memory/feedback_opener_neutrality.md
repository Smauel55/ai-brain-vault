REVISED 2026-06-11 (Samuel confirmed, after teacher meeting). The firewall narrowed from "no stance ever" to a person/group/third-rail ban, because AP Lang teaches persuasion and pointed rhetoric is the richest analysis material. Two modes now:

- **Neutral explainer (every tier, always safe):** report the issue and competing positions fairly, hand analysis to the student. ONLY mode on the non-civic tier.
- **Pointed persona advocacy (CIVIC tier only):** a piece MAY argue a position with full force IF (1) clear speaker named by ROLE not name (civilian to a city council, parent to a school board, resident to Congress about a bill; headnote names speaker/audience/occasion per [[feedback_persona_fabrication]] + Headnote-Spec — no invented proper names, no real people), (2) first-person EMOTION is encouraged (anger, grief, hope — the ethos/pathos the class analyzes), (3) the force aims at the ISSUE or INSTITUTION, never a person. "I'm furious this council voted to close our clinic" = in; "Councilman Reyes is a coward" = out.

**Three hard bans (BOTH modes, EVERY tier):** (1) no attack on a specific person — no living political figure as villain/hero/critical-addressee, even unnamed ("Mr. President" = the sitting president = banned); a real official may appear only as a neutral sourced actor ("the Secretary announced X"). (2) No attack on a group (party, religion, nationality, ideology, demographic). (3) No side on the national THIRD RAILS — abortion, guns, partisan elections/candidates, contested-value immigration (e.g. whether birthright citizenship should exist) — these stay neutral-explainer only even on the civic tier; birthright citizenship can run as a Ledger explaining mechanics, not a should-we argument.

**Test for what's unlockable on the civic tier:** value-consensus. If the shared value is broadly agreed and the dispute is over means/competence/local impact (local services, a bill's tradeoffs, school funding, infrastructure, governance, civic duty), pointed advocacy is fair game. If the dispute is over a contested value itself (third rails), stay neutral.

**Why:** product goes into classrooms under a teacher's name, across districts of every lean, in front of minors. A one-sided pathos piece on a third rail in an opposite-lean district is the existential trust/churn risk. The opposite-lean teacher is the test, not the sympathetic one. The narrowing keeps the rhetorical richness teachers want while protecting that teacher.

**How to apply / register routing:** Open Letter addressee and Reckoning culprit may now be an institution/body/company/practice (and pointed at it), but NEVER a named/identifiable politician or party, and never an argument for one side of a third rail. If the only honest target is a political figure or the only honest content is a third-rail stance, route to The Ledger or drop. Neutral path always exists via The Ledger; when in doubt route there.
