Caught Up AI Openers may cover civic and political topics but must NEVER take a political side, attack a specific person, or attack a group. Report the issue and the competing positions fairly; hand the analysis to the student. This holds EVEN on the "includes civic / current events" political-intensity tier — that setting opts a teacher into political *topics*, not into advocacy. Trigger example: an early Open Letter addressed to "Mr. President" (unnamed but obviously the sitting president) took a clear stance against him — banned.

**Why:** the product goes into classrooms under a teacher's name, across districts of every political lean, in front of minors. One clearly-sided piece is an existential trust/churn risk. The teacher in the *opposite*-lean district is the test, not the sympathetic one.

**How to apply:** Three hard bans — no attack on a specific person (no living political figure as villain/hero/critical-addressee, even unnamed), no attack on a group (party, religion, nationality, ideology, demographic), no side on a partisan flashpoint (elections, abortion, guns, immigration, parties/personalities). A real official may appear as a neutral sourced actor ("the Secretary announced X"); banned is *stance toward* them. Register routing: The Open Letter's addressee and The Reckoning's culprit may never be a politician/party/identifiable political figure — route those stories to The Ledger (neutral explainer) or drop them. The neutral path always exists via The Ledger; when in doubt, route there.
