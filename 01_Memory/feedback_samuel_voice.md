**Hard rules (never break):**
- No em-dashes. Ever. Zero appear across ~30 pages of his real writing. Use commas, semicolons, colons, parentheses, or start a new sentence.
- No "not just X but Y" framing
- No "It's not about X, it's about Y" closings
- No aphoristic wrap-up sentences that strain to mean something big
- No triplets with parallel structure (AI tell) — he prefers pairs
- Avoid stock phrases from his college essays (flagged as NOT his voice): "lit a fire in me," "scratched the surface," "smiling ear to ear," "buzz of," "endless possibilities," "well of passion waiting to be tapped," "stark contrast"

**Strong signatures (use these):**
- First-person commitment without hedging: "I believe..." "I think..." "I will..."
- Pairs over triplets: "Strength and confidence." "Self-worth and sincerity."
- Parenthetical asides for personality: "(but not really)" "(my previous knowledge came from Adventure Capitalist, which had nothing to do with venture capital)" — self-aware, dry, slightly self-deprecating
- Names failure or limitation out loud — never glosses limitations
- "Let's" and "So" for walk-through reasoning (conversational momentum)
- "Though" as mid- or end-of-clause hedge
- Opens with scene or specific observation, abstraction later
- Patient thinking-out-loud rhythm
- Specifics over abstractions: real names, real numbers, real failures

**Register for Caught Up AI materials:** between casual and reflective. Polished enough an AP Lang teacher trusts him, casual enough they hear an actual person. The casual register (Math IA) is closer to his actual voice than the polished college-essay register.

**What he does NOT like in copy:**
- AI-flavored writing (em-dashes, generic confidence)
- Salesy language or methodology-speak in teacher-facing materials
- Essay-like dense prose for pitch documents
- Assertive declarations of what teachers' problems are
- Soft promises that may not age well

**Why:** v0.1 of the one-pager was rejected as "sounds very much like AI." Voice profile built from four writing samples (chapel homily, Common App + supplements, Math IA on One Piece physics, Sense of Place essay on Zimbabwe).

**How to apply:** Before writing any customer-facing copy, read Samuel's Voice - Style Guide.md in the project folder. Read draft aloud — wherever it stumbles, that's an AI tell. Start from casual register and clean up rather than starting polished and trying to add personality.
