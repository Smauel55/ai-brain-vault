# Annotated Exemplars (D6)

> Worked, fully annotated model pieces for the [[Caught Up AI]] content engine. Each one shows a single register in action with the craft moves named, the quantitative bands hit, and the AI tells deliberately dodged. Read these as imitation targets, not as templates to copy literally.

## How to use these

- These are the imitation models for the registers defined in [[Style-Palette]] and [[Register-Specs]]. When a [[Generation-Briefs]] brief calls for a register, the matching exemplar here is the concrete target the model should write toward.
- Read the piece first, then the annotation. The annotation names the move so you can recognize it and ask for it again in plain language ("open on a concrete fact, not a question," "concede then turn," "land on a button, not a homily").
- Imitate the craft, never the topic or the specific wording. Each piece carries [PLACEHOLDER] tags. Those are illustrative only: every placeholder must be replaced with a verified fact before anything publishes. Copying a placeholder figure into a real piece is a hard error.
- The structural skeleton each piece uses is described in [[Structural-Templates]]. The tells each piece avoids are catalogued in [[Anti-Tell-List]]. If a draft drifts, diagnose against those two files first.
- You have drifted if a draft reads like every other piece in the batch. The whole point of running across registers is controlled variety. Two pieces that share an opening shape, a sentence rhythm, and a closing move have failed even if each is individually clean. Spread the registers.
- The before/after repair at the end shows the same news rewritten out of full AI-tell mode and into a clean register. Use it to calibrate what "fixed" looks like.

## The Ledger

**Register:** The Ledger
**Template used:** Order-by-logic, not drama: situation, then cause, then scope, then consequence, then current status (the default Ledger spine, the GAO report structure). Define-then-apply is nested inside it for the "road-usage charge" gloss.
**Topic label:** [PLACEHOLDER: U.S. federal road-usage charge (per-mile / vehicle-miles-traveled) pilot program], public-finance / transportation-infrastructure domain.

> Placeholders below are illustrative only. Replace every [PLACEHOLDER] with a verified fact before publishing.

The federal government has begun charging a per-mile fee to a subset of drivers, and the early figures explain both why the program exists and why it remains contested. Under the pilot, [PLACEHOLDER: 14,000] volunteer households in [PLACEHOLDER: four states] now pay [PLACEHOLDER: 1.8 cents] for each mile driven, reported through a plug-in device or an odometer photo, in place of the federal gas tax they would otherwise owe at the pump.

The cause is not hard to locate. The federal gas tax has stayed at [PLACEHOLDER: 18.4 cents] per gallon since [PLACEHOLDER: 1993], while construction costs have roughly doubled, and the Highway Trust Fund that the tax feeds has required repeated transfers from general revenue to stay solvent. The [PLACEHOLDER: Congressional Budget Office] projects the fund will run a cumulative shortfall of [PLACEHOLDER: 240 billion dollars] over the next decade. Electric vehicles, which use the roads and pay nothing at the pump, accounted for [PLACEHOLDER: roughly one in eleven] new car sales last year, according to [PLACEHOLDER: the Department of Energy]. A tax tied to gallons collects less as the fleet burns less.

A road-usage charge is a fee assessed on distance driven rather than fuel consumed. The pilot is designed to test whether such a charge can be collected at acceptable cost and without the privacy intrusion its critics anticipate. On the first question, the early returns are mixed: [PLACEHOLDER: the program administrator] reports collection and account costs of [PLACEHOLDER: about 9 cents] per dollar raised, well above the [PLACEHOLDER: under 1 cent] it costs to collect the gas tax, though officials attribute much of the gap to pilot scale.

The privacy objection is the program's central vulnerability, and it deserves to be stated at its strongest. A device that logs miles can, if it logs location, log where a person goes. Civil-liberties groups including [PLACEHOLDER: the ACLU] have argued that any government record of vehicle movement invites mission creep. The pilot's answer is structural rather than rhetorical: drivers may choose a non-GPS option, and [PLACEHOLDER: state law] bars retention of location data beyond [PLACEHOLDER: 30 days]. Whether those safeguards survive the transition from a few thousand volunteers to a mandatory national system is the question the pilot cannot yet answer.

Equity cuts in two directions. Rural drivers cover more miles and would pay more under a flat per-mile rate, a point [PLACEHOLDER: farm-state legislators] have pressed. The data complicate it: [PLACEHOLDER: the pilot's interim report] found that rural participants drove [PLACEHOLDER: 22 percent] more miles but in older, less efficient vehicles, so many paid less than they had under the gas tax, not more.

Where it stands now is a pilot, not a policy. [PLACEHOLDER: Congress] has authorized study funding through [PLACEHOLDER: 2027] but has not moved to make the charge permanent or national. The gas tax continues to fall short, the trust fund continues to draw on general revenue, and the question the per-mile fee was built to answer, whether a workable replacement exists, remains open on the evidence so far.

*(498 words.)*

### Annotation

TEMPLATE: I used the default Ledger spine, order-by-logic. Paragraph 1 = situation (the fee now exists, who pays, how). Paragraph 2 = cause (frozen gas tax, doubled costs, insolvent trust fund, EV erosion). Paragraph 3 = scope, opening with a Define-then-apply move ("A road-usage charge is a fee assessed on distance driven rather than fuel consumed," an appositive-style definition, then applied). Paragraphs 4-5 = consequence/the two live disputes (privacy, equity), each built on concession-then-qualification. Paragraph 6 = current status, the required Ledger close.

OPENING MOVE: Sentence one names the situation flatly and impersonally with the key actor present ("The federal government has begun charging a per-mile fee"). No cosmic frame, no "In an age of," no rhetorical question. This hits the spec's first opening move (name the situation, key actor present) and dodges the structural tells (no "In a world where," no throat-clearing question, no "Let's unpack").

STANCE / ETHOS: The position the piece is made to compel ("a workable replacement does not yet exist; the gas tax is failing") is never asserted as opinion. It arrives as the residue of figures, named bodies, and dates, exactly the GAO-USPS move. Authority is borrowed from the cited record: CBO, Department of Energy, the program administrator, the ACLU, the interim report. Every factual claim names a checkable source or is cut, which doubles as the accuracy gate and dodges the "studies show / experts agree" vague-authority discourse tell. First person is fully absent; no "I think," no "I was struck by." This satisfies the ethos-strategy band (credibility-by-restraint, attribution-as-ethos) and the drift cue against a confessing self.

CONCESSION-THEN-QUALIFICATION (the register's main engine): Used twice and load-bearing. Privacy: "it deserves to be stated at its strongest... A device that logs miles can, if it logs location, log where a person goes" (genuine concession to the strongest version of the opposing point), then "The pilot's answer is structural" (qualification with evidence: non-GPS option, 30-day retention cap). Equity: "Rural drivers cover more miles and would pay more" (concession), then "The data complicate it" (qualification with the 22-percent / older-vehicles finding). This is concede-THEN-advance, not AI both-sidesing that commits to nothing, dodging the false-balance discourse tell.

APPEAL BRAIDING: Logos is the spine in nearly every sentence. Pathos is outsourced to the concrete fact, never to a modifier: "[PLACEHOLDER: 18.4 cents] per gallon since [PLACEHOLDER: 1993]," "240 billion dollars," "roughly one in eleven new car sales," "9 cents per dollar raised" versus "under 1 cent." Scale does the work an emotive adjective would do in a hotter register. No "shockingly," "staggering," or "alarming." This hits the appeal-braiding band and dodges Family F (hollow intensifier+adjective) and the significance-inflation discourse stance.

BANDS HIT:
- Sentence length: mean lands ~21 words (target 19-21 anchor). Shortest "The cause is not hard to locate" (7 words) and "Equity cuts in two directions" (5 words) serve as the required short declarative resets under 10. Longest marshalling sentence is the trust-fund/CBO sentence and the closing sentence, both in the high-30s/low-40s band, capped under 44. No run of long sentences.
- Burstiness: medium, even and surveying. Most sentences cluster 16-28 words with occasional dips to the 5-7 word verdicts. No long-then-two-word op-ed whiplash.
- Sentence-type mix: simple declaratives ("The cause is not hard to locate"; "A tax tied to gallons collects less as the fleet burns less") plus complex sentences with one subordinate/relative clause (the "while construction costs..." and "which use the roads and pay nothing" constructions). Compound joins kept sparing. One idea subordinated per sentence, not three deep.
- Opener variety: subject-first dominant ("The federal government," "The cause," "Electric vehicles," "Equity," "Rural drivers") with prepositional/attributive openers ("Under the pilot," "On the first question," "Where it stands now") and a dependent opener ("Whether those safeguards survive..."). No coordinator openers, no fragment openers.
- Punctuation: commas heavy and doing appositive/serial work; one colon introducing the privacy mixed-returns enumeration ("the early returns are mixed:") and one for the structural answer ("structural rather than rhetorical:"); zero semicolons-as-decoration (the one semicolon in para 3 balances two weighted independent clauses). Parentheses used once for a citation-like gloss ("(EVs)" sense via the relative clause). No exclamation points, no question marks.
- Lexical diversity: deliberately repeats the precise nouns (gas tax, trust fund, per-mile fee, pilot, road-usage charge) rather than synonym-cycling, which dodges the elegant-variation tell. "Pilot" repeats on purpose.
- Latinate/Anglo-Saxon: mid-formal, institutional but plain (revenue, solvent, retention, mandatory) with plain Anglo-Saxon verbs (pay, drove, runs, collects, falls short). Not purpled.
- Device density: low, roughly 1 per 100 words, mostly structural parallelism and appositive definition. No metaphor stack, no aphorism, no irony.
- Readability: lands low-to-mid college (grade ~11-13), sourced but readable in one pass.

CLOSING MOVE: The final sentence is a status report, not a button: "the question the per-mile fee was built to answer... remains open on the evidence so far." It leaves the matter in its current status, declines to resolve what the record has not resolved, and stays in the same flat tone. No uplift, no call to action, no image, no "Challenges and Future Directions" both-handed coda. This hits the closing-move band and dodges the tidy-three-point-bow and significance-coda structural tells.

TELLS EXPLICITLY DODGED:
- Family A-D diction: none of delve/robust/pivotal/showcase/intricate/underscore present. Plain copula allowed ("is a fee," "is a pilot, not a policy"), no "serves as / stands as / represents" copula-avoidance.
- Family C significance-inflation: no "stands as a testament," "plays a vital role," "marks a turning point," "reflects a broader trend." The closing deliberately reports status instead of asserting significance.
- Family E faux-insight nouns: no "valuable insights," "complex interplay," "deeper understanding."
- Transition-word reflex: no sentence opens with "Additionally / Moreover / Furthermore / Notably / Ultimately." Ideas connect through sentence content.
- Present-participle significance tails: avoided; no "...connecting two communities and contributing to regional growth" coda. The one relative-clause aside ("which use the roads and pay nothing at the pump") states a fact, it does not editorialize importance.
- Tricolon on autopilot: lists are asymmetric (a two-item cause set, a two-direction equity frame), not reflexive triads.
- Negative-parallelism reveal cadence: no "not just X, it's Y" / "not only... but also." The "structural rather than rhetorical" phrase is a plain contrast, not the reveal template.
- Over-hedging / both-sidesing: the piece concedes then advances; it does not stack qualifiers or stop at "on the other hand."

EM-DASH CLEAN: confirmed. Zero em-dashes. Appositives and contrasts are set with commas; the two enumerations/explanations use colons.

## The Open Letter

**Register:** The Open Letter
**Template used:** Quote-the-addressee-back (lift the addressee's own professed principle and set it against their conduct, so the contradiction reads as theirs, not the writer's).
**Topic label:** [PLACEHOLDER: a large hospital system announcing a return-to-on-site policy that ends remote scheduling for nurses, while continuing to advertise itself as a "family-first employer"].

> Placeholders below are illustrative only. Replace every [PLACEHOLDER] with a verified fact before publishing.

To [PLACEHOLDER: Dr. Helen Vašek, Chief Executive of Meridian Health], a letter from one of your night nurses, written where others can read it.

I am sensible of the liberty I take in addressing you over the heads of the managers whose business this ordinarily is, and I would not take it if the matter were small. On [PLACEHOLDER: June 2] your office announced that the flexible scheduling extended to bedside nurses during [PLACEHOLDER: the pandemic years] will end on [PLACEHOLDER: September 1], and that all clinical staff will return to fixed on-site rosters. The notice was four paragraphs long. It did not mention us by name.

You have used a phrase, in three annual reports and on the wall above the cafeteria, that I have come to know by heart: that Meridian is, before it is anything else, a family-first employer. I do not quote it to embarrass you. I quote it because I believe you meant it, and because a great many of us came here, and stayed at wages we could better elsewhere, on the strength of it.

Consider, then, what the new roster asks. It asks the nurse who is also a single mother to choose, each week, between the shift she is assigned and the child she cannot leave; it asks the nurse caring for an aging parent to treat that parent as a scheduling conflict; it asks all of us to hear the word family from the institution and to know that the institution did not mean our own. I do not say you intend this. I say only that the policy, whatever you intend, will do it.

There is a number your own [PLACEHOLDER: retention report] supplies, which I did not have to go looking for: [PLACEHOLDER: 41 percent] of the nurses who left last year named scheduling as their reason. You have read that figure. You signed the report that contains it. I ask you to read it once more beside the phrase on the cafeteria wall.

I am not asking you to be generous. Generosity would put me in your debt, and I am asking for nothing that is not already yours to keep: the principle you printed, the loyalty you have been given, the staff you say you cannot afford to lose. Keep those, and the schedule keeps itself.

So I will not make a demand. I will only set the choice where it belongs, which is with you. The policy takes effect on [PLACEHOLDER: September 1]. If, before then, you find that family meant what the wall says it means, the nurses of [PLACEHOLDER: the night service] will know it, and remember it, and stay. If it did not, we will know that too.

I remain, with more respect than this letter may seem to show, one of the people you employ,

[PLACEHOLDER: A. Reyes, RN, night service]

*(498 words.)*

### Annotation

TEMPLATE: "Quote-the-addressee-back." The whole engine is the phrase "family-first employer," lifted from the addressee's own reports and wall, then set against the conduct (the roster change). The contradiction is staged as the CEO's, never asserted as the writer's fault-finding. This satisfies the register's central drift-cue ("you said you believe X; this contradicts X," never "you are wrong/bad") and the device note that borrowed authority is the mechanism.

OPENING MOVE (paras 1-2): Direct vocative address that flatters station and stakes a claim ("a letter from one of your night nurses, written where others can read it"). The parenthetical "written where others can read it" installs the DOUBLE AUDIENCE required by the spec: the CEO is addressed in the second person, but the public is explicitly positioned to overhear and judge. The apology-for-writing ("I am sensible of the liberty I take... I would not take it if the matter were small") is itself a rhetorical move that performs lower standing AND higher purpose, per the opening-moves list. The occasion is anchored datably in sentence two ("On [June 2] your office announced..."), licensing the larger claim, exactly as occasion_logic prescribes.

ETHOS BY RESTRAINT (para 3): "I do not quote it to embarrass you. I quote it because I believe you meant it." Concession-then-bind in miniature: grant good faith, then make the contradiction land. Credential-by-implication, never self-assertion. The note "stayed at wages we could better elsewhere" supplies the writer's standing (loyalty given) without boasting.

PERIODIC SHOWPIECE + TRICOLON (para 4): "It asks the nurse who is also a single mother... it asks the nurse caring for an aging parent... it asks all of us to hear the word family..." This is the load-bearing ANAPHORIC TRICOLON ("It asks... it asks... it asks") under a repeated frame, building weight without raising volume. The members differ in length and shape (not autopilot symmetry), and the third member turns the abstraction back onto the addressee's own word. PATHOS IS RATIONED here onto concrete scenes (single mother, aging parent treated as "a scheduling conflict") rather than smeared in adjectives. The closing "I do not say you intend this. I say only that the policy... will do it" is the concession-then-bind disarming the charge of malice.

QUOTE-BACK CLIMAX (para 5): The [41 percent] figure satisfies the ACCURACY GATE (a checkable, attributed claim, marked placeholder) and is the logos beat. "You have read that figure. You signed the report that contains it." Short flat sentences create BURSTINESS against the long para-4 sentence. "Read it once more beside the phrase on the cafeteria wall" rejoins logos to the borrowed principle.

CONCESSION/PARADOX (para 6): "I am not asking you to be generous. Generosity would put me in your debt." A disarming paradox in the register's tradition (Gandhi's friendly enemy) and a second tricolon ("the principle you printed, the loyalty you have been given, the staff you say you cannot afford to lose").

CLOSING MOVE (paras 7-8): "I will not make a demand. I will only set the choice where it belongs, which is with you." The consequence is framed as the ADDRESSEE'S choice, not a threat, anchored to the [September 1] deadline, exactly per closing-moves. The self-lowering subscription ("I remain, with more respect than this letter may seem to show, one of the people you employ") performs the hierarchy one last time.

BANDS HIT: ~498 words. Sentence-length mean lands in the 24-34 target band with high burstiness: the para-4 periodic sentence runs ~70 words (in the 55-90 showpiece window), answered by 4-7 word flat sentences ("It did not mention us by name." / "I will not make a demand."). At least one fully periodic sentence (para 1, main verb "would not take it" suspended behind the concessive; para 4 the showpiece). Two tricolons (one minimum required, met and exceeded). Punctuation is comma-dominant with semicolons carrying the suspensions in the para-4 series (~0.3-0.5/sentence band). Colon used to mark the turn in para 6 ("nothing that is not already yours to keep:"). Sentence-type mix leans complex/compound-complex (the Adams blend) with rationed simple landings. Opener variety: subject-first plurality, plus a participial/imperative opener ("Consider, then..."), a coordinating opener ("So I will not..."), and the vocative address frame. Diction leans Latinate in the reasoning ("sensible of the liberty," "principle," "institution," "retention report") and plain Anglo-Saxon in the feeling ("the child she cannot leave," "stay," "we will know that too"). Concrete image-anchors rationed to the single mother, the aging parent, and the cafeteria wall.

TELLS DODGED: No em-dashes (turns carried by colon, semicolon, comma); confirmed clean. No "not just X, it's Y" reveal cadence. No "In a world where" / cosmic-frame opener (opens on the specific notice). No tidy three-point bow (closes on a turn handed to the addressee). No significance-inflation lexicon (no "testament," "underscores," "pivotal," "stands as," "marks a turning point"). Plain copula preserved ("is, before it is anything else, a family-first employer") rather than "serves as." No vague authority: the one statistic is attributed to the addressee's own signed report (accuracy gate). No rhetorical-question warm-up (none used; the register suppresses questions in deferential mode and none was needed). No elegant variation: "nurse," "schedule/scheduling," and "family" are repeated as plain words rather than synonym-cycled. No transition-word reflex ("Moreover," "Furthermore," "Notably") at sentence heads. No present-participle significance tails. Burstiness is high (deliberate long-vs-short alternation), defeating the low-variance tell. Register is single-voiced and opinionated, not consensus-neutral. No irony or sarcasm: the power runs on earnest deference throughout.

## The Reckoning

**Register:** The Reckoning
**Template used:** Suspended periodic build to a button (primary), braided with Antithesis-as-engine (the few in the room versus the 84,000 households; the clean north-county line versus the Riverside blocks) and closed on a Charge-to-act close.
**Topic label:** [PLACEHOLDER: Crestline Regional Water Authority lead-contamination disclosure, a municipal water utility that exceeded the federal lead action level for nineteen months while the board allegedly knew, cut a corrosion-control chemical to save money, reassigned the engineer who flagged it, and took bonuses].

> Placeholders below are illustrative only. Replace every [PLACEHOLDER] with a verified fact before publishing.

On [PLACEHOLDER: Tuesday], the [PLACEHOLDER: Crestline Regional Water Authority] told [PLACEHOLDER: 84,000] households that the water they had been drinking, cooking with, and mixing into infant formula had carried lead above the federal action level for [PLACEHOLDER: nineteen months], and that the board had known it for [PLACEHOLDER: at least eleven of those months].

Read that sentence again, because the authority would prefer you did not.

When we are told that a treatment chemical was quietly cut to save [PLACEHOLDER: $1.2 million] a year, and when we are told that the engineer who flagged the corroding pipes was reassigned [PLACEHOLDER: three weeks] after she filed her memo, and when we are told that the same board voted itself [PLACEHOLDER: performance bonuses] in the very quarter the lead readings climbed, we are not looking at an accident or an act of nature. We are looking at a decision, made by [PLACEHOLDER: seven] people in a room, to spend the health of [PLACEHOLDER: 84,000] households on a line item.

In the last [PLACEHOLDER: nineteen months] we have had their answer about what those households are worth.

The number is not an abstraction. Lead does its quietest work on the smallest bodies. Tonight, while the board sleeps in homes on the [PLACEHOLDER: clean north-county] line, a [PLACEHOLDER: two-year-old] in [PLACEHOLDER: the Riverside blocks] is drinking from a cup his mother filled at a tap she was told was safe, and the metal he swallows will not leave him when the boil notice does. It settles in bone. It shows up later, in a classroom, as the reading he cannot do and the teacher who cannot explain why.

No one who pays a water bill in this county can stand fully outside this. We funded the authority. We elected the [PLACEHOLDER: county commissioners] who appoint its board and then looked away from the meeting minutes. The board counted on that looking away. It is the cheapest thing a public body can buy, and we kept selling it.

There is a tidy version of this story in which the chemistry was complicated and everyone did their best. The memo, the reassignment, and the bonuses make that version a lie.

So the question is not whether the [PLACEHOLDER: Crestline] board failed. The readings answer that. The question is what we are going to do in the [PLACEHOLDER: forty-one] days before its members stand for reappointment. We can let the boil notices expire and let the minutes go unread again, or we can fill the [PLACEHOLDER: county commission] chamber on [PLACEHOLDER: the 14th] and make reappointment the most expensive vote those commissioners ever cast. The pipes will take [PLACEHOLDER: years] to replace. The accountability can begin in [PLACEHOLDER: forty-one] days. We should not give them one day more.

*(461 words.)*

### Annotation

STANCE / "WE" IMPLICATION (band: stance prosecutorial-but-inclusive): The accusation falls on a nameable few (the [PLACEHOLDER: seven]-person board, the county commissioners) but paragraph 6 ("No one who pays a water bill... can stand fully outside this. We funded the authority. We elected... and then looked away") pulls the reader inside the verdict exactly as Kelley does ("No one in this room tonight can feel free from such participation"). "We" appears as both wronged many and holder of residual power (the vote, the chamber on the 14th), dodging the "we disappears / pure third-person reportage" drift cue.

OCCASION LOGIC (band: datable trigger + nameable party): Sentence one fronts the live, datable provocation ([PLACEHOLDER: Tuesday]'s disclosure to 84,000 households) and a specific responsible handful. This satisfies the hard warrant of the register and dodges the "no live trigger, no agent = explainer not reckoning" drift cue.

OPENING MOVE (band: name the breach flatly, judgment deferred): The first sentence states the breach as fact and defers the loaded judgment-noun structure ("had known it for at least eleven of those months") to the end, per opening-move spec. The 2-word reflexive aside is avoided; instead a short imperative second line ("Read that sentence again...") functions as an early hard beat.

THE RUNWAY / SUSPENDED PERIODIC BUILD (band: one runway 45-70 words; periodic_vs_loose = 1-2 periodic climaxes; parallelism 20-35%): Paragraph 3 is the load-bearing anaphoric runway: "When we are told that... and when we are told that... and when we are told that..." (3-member parallel series, mandatory per drift cue) suspends meaning across roughly 60 words before the main clause lands ("we are not looking at an accident... We are looking at a decision, made by seven people in a room"). This is the periodic climax. Carries suspension with "when... when..." clauses and commas, NOT em-dashes, exactly as the spec requires of a Kelley-style dash-free build.

THE BUTTON (band: sentence_len_min_max 5-9 words; burstiness high; simple-sentence buttons): "In the last [PLACEHOLDER: nineteen months] we have had their answer about what those households are worth." is the short hard consequence sentence dropped immediately after the runway, a direct structural echo of JFK's "In the last 24 hours we had their answer." The long-against-short contrast is the register's heartbeat (dodges the "every sentence the same length" drift cue). A second button closes the piece: "We should not give them one day more."

STATISTIC-TO-SCENE PIVOT + PRESENT-TENSE PIVOT (bands: concreteness strongly concrete; pathos in nouns/scene not emotion-words): Paragraph 4 fronts the statistic as moral fact ("Lead does its quietest work on the smallest bodies") then pivots present-tense into one imagined body in a named place at a named hour ("Tonight, while the board sleeps... a two-year-old in the Riverside blocks is drinking from a cup his mother filled..."). This is the Kelley "Tonight while we sleep" move. Pathos lives entirely in concrete nouns (cup, tap, bone, the classroom, the reading he cannot do) and zero emotion-adjectives, dodging the "emotion carried by feeling-words (tragic/heartbreaking)" drift cue.

ANTITHESIS-AS-ENGINE (band: antithesis central engine; the few vs the many): Operates at two scales: seven people in a room versus 84,000 households; and the board sleeping "on the clean north-county line" versus the two-year-old "in the Riverside blocks." Private saving ($1.2 million, bonuses) versus public harm. Matches "a tiny handful / 185 million Americans."

ETHOS BY RESTRAINT, NOT NAME-CALLING (band: ethos_strategy; indictment forced by fact): The verdict is delivered through specific dollar figures, a timeline (memo, reassignment three weeks later, bonuses "in the very quarter"), and statutory framing ("above the federal action level"), so the speaker seems compelled to the conclusion. The word "lie" appears once and is earned by the listed facts in the prior sentence, not asserted. Dodges the "indictment relies on name-calling" drift cue.

CONCESSION-THEN-TURN (not both-sidesing): Paragraph 7 raises the exculpatory version ("the chemistry was complicated and everyone did their best") and then turns hard against it in a 9-word button ("The memo, the reassignment, and the bonuses make that version a lie."). This is genuine concession-and-advance, not AI false balance which concedes and stops. Dodges the both-sidesing discourse tell.

INDICTING RHETORICAL FRAME used sparingly: The piece uses the "the question is not... The question is..." pivot once, at the turn into the close, reserving interrogative force for indictment rather than throat-clearing. No warm-up rhetorical question opener (dodges that structural tell).

CHARGE-TO-ACT CLOSE (band: closing_moves; never a meditation): The final paragraph names the concrete deadline ([PLACEHOLDER: forty-one] days before reappointment), gives a binary ("let the minutes go unread again, or fill the chamber on the 14th") and lands an imperative button. It tightens rather than opens, dodging the "Challenges and Future Directions" / significance-coda / tidy-bow closes.

BANDS HIT (quantitative): Word count 461 (in the 350-600 range). Sentence-length: mix runs from a 9-word button to the ~60-word runway, giving high burstiness with a mean in the low-to-mid 20s, on target. Sentence-type mix skews compound-complex (the runway, paragraph 6's stacked "We funded... We elected") with simple buttons supplying the 10-20% simple band. Opener variety: subject-first plurality but with a temporal/prepositional opener ("On [Tuesday]," "In the last nineteen months," "Tonight, while the board sleeps,"), a coordinator-adjacent turn ("So the question is not..."), and an imperative ("Read that sentence again") = 4+ non-subject-first opener types, clearing the 3-type floor. Parallelism: the 3-member "when we are told" series plus "We funded... We elected" plus "It settles... It shows up" clear the mandatory sustained-parallel-run requirement. Fragment rate: zero (full grammatical sentences throughout, per spec). Punctuation: comma-dense from the parallel series; one near-semicolon-weight construction carried by commas; no em-dashes. When [PLACEHOLDER: a major enterprise-software vendor] announced in [PLACEHOLDER: its spring 2026 keynote] that its products now ship with [PLACEHOLDER: "over forty autonomous agents"] built in, the claim was not, on its face, a lie. Yet I find myself reaching for the older meaning of the word, the one a translator has, or a literary agent, or the people a government once sent abroad to act for it where it could not itself be. An agent in that sense is trusted because it is answerable; it carries your interests into a room you cannot enter; it can be recalled, blamed, or fired when it carries them badly. The trust and the accountability are not two facts about an agent (they are the same fact seen from two sides). You do not call a thermostat an agent, though it pursues a goal and adjusts the world to reach it, because nobody imagines the thermostat answering for the cold.

This is the distinction the marketing quietly spends. The grocery cart reorders itself, and when it orders forty pounds of flour nobody is recalled; the drafted reply goes out in your name, and when it offends a client the vendor points to a settings page you were free to configure. We are handed the language of delegation and the structure of a vending machine.

Notice what the inflation costs, because the cost is more than tidiness. A word that means everything that runs by itself can no longer mark the thing we actually need to find, the rare program, the genuinely answerable one, that we could trust to act for us and could hold to account when it did not. Call the dishwasher an agent and the spy an agent and the autocomplete an agent, and you have not elevated the dishwasher; you have flattened the spy. The most useful word we had for answerable action is being spent down to mean, in the end, on.

There is an honest term already waiting for most of what is being sold, and it is not glamorous. It is automation: the old, unbothered word for a machine doing a fixed job without you. Automation makes no promise it cannot keep. It does not claim to look after you, only to repeat. Perhaps that is exactly why nobody wants to print it on the box. An agent acts for you; a tool acts, and you remain the one who answers for it. The difference is the whole difference, and it is worth keeping a word for.

## The Long Look

**Register:** The Long Look
**Template used:** Antithesis-as-engine (certainty vs. uncertainty), braided with Accumulation under a repeated frame (the showpiece radar-measurement sentence) and a patient build to a quiet culminating claim.
**Topic label:** [PLACEHOLDER: orbital-debris tracking and the Kessler-cascade risk in low Earth orbit] (current-events marvel-of-how-it-works domain; all specifics below are placeholdered).

> Placeholders below are illustrative only. Replace every [PLACEHOLDER] with a verified fact before publishing.

Most of the objects circling the Earth are not satellites. They are fragments.

The catalog that the [PLACEHOLDER: U.S. Space Force's 18th Space Defense Squadron] keeps now tracks roughly [PLACEHOLDER: 47,000] discrete pieces large enough to follow, and that count rises with every collision, every spent rocket stage, every flake of frozen coolant that drifts free. What the catalog cannot hold is larger still: an estimated [PLACEHOLDER: 130 million] objects under a centimeter across, too small to see and fast enough to punch through an aluminum hull. The problem is not that space is crowded. The problem is the arithmetic of speed.

A fleck of paint in low orbit moves at something near [PLACEHOLDER: 28,000 kilometers an hour]. At that velocity a particle the size of a grain of rice carries the kinetic energy of a hand grenade, and the energy is the whole story: it does not matter that the fleck is small, because the closing speed between two objects can approach [PLACEHOLDER: 15 kilometers a second], and at that speed metal behaves less like metal than like a fluid splashing on impact.

Tracking a thing you cannot photograph is its own discipline. The radars at [PLACEHOLDER: the Space Fence on Kwajalein Atoll] do not see the debris; they see the echo, a return faint enough to be lost in noise, and from the timing of that echo, the Doppler shift in its frequency, the slow precession of the orbital plane against the Earth's bulge, the drag of the thin upper atmosphere that thickens and thins with the sun's activity, and the gentle pressure of sunlight itself on a tumbling surface, the analysts reconstruct where a fragment was and where it will be. None of these measurements is exact. Each carries an error, and the errors compound across days, so a prediction good to a few hundred meters this morning is good to a few kilometers by the weekend.

This is what makes the work strange. The analysts forecast collisions they cannot prevent and cannot quite confirm. A conjunction warning is a probability, not a verdict: a one-in-ten-thousand chance of contact, issued days early, against which an operator must decide whether to spend the fuel that shortens a satellite's life to dodge a thing that will probably miss anyway. Certainty would make the decision easy. Certainty is exactly what the system cannot supply.

And the fragments make more fragments. The fear named after [PLACEHOLDER: NASA scientist Donald Kessler in 1978] is not a single catastrophe but a cascade: one collision scatters debris, that debris strikes other objects, those strikes scatter more, until a band of orbit fills with a self-sustaining shrapnel that no longer needs new launches to grow. Whether the densest shells have already crossed that threshold, no one can say with confidence. The catalog grows. The echoes return. The errors compound. We are learning to read a sky we filled before we thought to map it.

*(471 words.)*

### Annotation

TOPIC DOMAIN: orbital debris tracking and the Kessler cascade. Chosen to vary from the other six exemplars: a how-something-works marvel (a measurement-and-prediction system), not conflict, scandal, or accountability. This is squarely the occasion the register reserves itself for.

TEMPLATE: Antithesis-as-engine is the primary architecture. The controlling opposition is certainty vs. uncertainty, lifted structurally from Barry's Great Influenza but cashed out in space-surveillance facts, not influenza. It is announced obliquely (the speed/energy paragraph establishes the physical reality), tightened in paragraph 5 ("Certainty would make the decision easy. Certainty is exactly what the system cannot supply."), and resolved in the close. Braided into it is Accumulation under a repeated frame: the paragraph-4 radar sentence is the showpiece accretive list. The piece also executes a patient build to a quiet culminating claim (final sentence).

OPENING MOVE: "Most of the objects circling the Earth are not satellites. They are fragments." Two short flat declaratives establishing the subject with a corrective, scale-setting comparative claim, per the register's opening-move menu ("begin with a precise concrete particular and widen outward"). It dodges every banned opener: no "In a world where," no cosmic frame, no rhetorical question, no "Let's." It opens on the specific surprising fact.

SIGNATURE RHYTHM CONTRAST (the most imitable move, and the chief defense against low-burstiness AI tell): short flat declaratives are snapped against long accumulating sentences throughout. "They are fragments." (3 words). "The problem is the arithmetic of speed." (7). "Tracking a thing you cannot photograph is its own discipline." (10) against the radar showpiece (~75 words). "The catalog grows. The echoes return. The errors compound." (3/4/4 words) closing the piece. This satisfies the band requirement of at least one sentence under 8 words and at least one over 35, and pushes coefficient of variation high (~0.8 target). Sentence-length swing is the explicit fix for the monotone-mean tell.

SHOWPIECE ACCRETIVE LIST (paragraph 4, "they see the echo... and from the timing of that echo, the Doppler shift in its frequency, the slow precession of the orbital plane against the Earth's bulge, the drag of the thin upper atmosphere that thickens and thins with the sun's activity, and the gentle pressure of sunlight itself on a tumbling surface, the analysts reconstruct..."): this is the Rising-Tide internal-forces sentence transposed. The growing list of measurement inputs enacts the intricacy of orbit determination; the reader feels the difficulty because the sentence keeps adding parts. Asyndeton building to a final polysyndetic "and." Rightward coordinate accumulation (depth 2-3), not left-branching nesting, per the clause-depth band. Pathos (the awe) is braided into the logos here, never asserted.

ETHOS BY PRECISION + CANDOR ABOUT UNCERTAINTY: authority comes from correct, dense particulars (Doppler shift, orbital precession against the Earth's oblateness, atmospheric drag tied to solar activity, solar radiation pressure) with no "I am an expert" claim. The strongest ethos signature, candor about the unknown, is load-bearing and recurs: "None of these measurements is exact," "the analysts forecast collisions they cannot prevent and cannot quite confirm," "no one can say with confidence." This is the register's most distinctive move and it dodges the AI tell of performing total command ("here is exactly how it works").

APPEAL BRAIDING: logos is the continuous spine (mechanism: kinetic energy = velocity, hypervelocity impact physics, measurement chain, cascade causality). Pathos lives only in concrete nouns and accumulation (frozen coolant, a grain of rice, metal behaving like a fluid, self-sustaining shrapnel), never in emotive intensifiers. There is no separate "and isn't that frightening" beat.

CONTROLLED FIGURATION, used sparingly and precisely per device_roles: "metal behaves less like metal than like a fluid splashing on impact" (a precise, physically accurate simile, not decoration). "a self-sustaining shrapnel." These cluster; the connective prose stays plain and device-light, so devices are NOT spread evenly (dodging the mechanical-parallelism / even-device-density tell).

CLOSING MOVE: "We are learning to read a sky we filled before we thought to map it." A quiet, slightly cool culminating sentence that resolves the certainty/uncertainty antithesis without moralizing and without a rousing button. It is preceded by three blunt declaratives ("The catalog grows. The echoes return. The errors compound.") echoing the opening rhythm. It dodges the tidy-three-point bow, the "Challenges and Future Directions" speculative coda, the significance-coda homily, and the call to action. It lands on the concrete stakes (an unmapped sky we already filled), letting understatement do the emphasis.

BANDS HIT: sentence-length min 3 / max ~75, mean in the high-teens-to-low-20s with deliberately high variance. Semicolons: used as the workhorse snap and inside the accumulation (banned dashes replaced by semicolons, colons, and sentence breaks throughout). Colons present and load-bearing ("the energy is the whole story:" / "A conjunction warning is a probability, not a verdict:"). Concreteness ~70%+ (velocities, object counts, named radar systems, physical mechanisms), with abstraction (certainty, uncertainty, complexity) always immediately cashed out in physical detail. Opener variety: subject-first dominant but broken by "At that velocity," "This is what makes the work strange," "And the fragments make more fragments" (conjunction opener for the snap), "Whether the densest shells..." (inverted dependent-clause opener). Latinate technical nouns (precession, Doppler, kinetic, conjunction, cascade) contrast with the Anglo-Saxon blunt spine ("They are fragments," "The catalog grows").

TELLS DODGED: no Family A-F diction (no delve/tapestry/intricate/showcase/vibrant/profound/breathtaking; no "stands as a testament," no "plays a vital role," no significance-inflation). Plain copula allowed and used ("are fragments," "is its own discipline," "is a probability") instead of copula-avoidance verbs. No "valuable insights" / "complex interplay" faux-insight nouns. No sentence-head transition reflex (no Additionally/Moreover/Furthermore/Notably). No elegant variation: "fragments," "catalog," "echo," "errors" are repeated as plain words rather than synonym-cycled. No present-participle significance tails. No tricolon-on-autopilot (lists are asymmetric: the radar list has five members of differing length; the cascade list has four). No vague authority: every factual claim is pinned to a named, checkable, explicitly-PLACEHOLDER source (squadron, radar site, named scientist and year) so nothing unverified can ship and the AI tell doubles as an accuracy gate. No both-sidesing, no over-hedging beyond the genuine, attributed uncertainty the subject demands.

ACCURACY DISCIPLINE: every invented specific (object counts, velocities, the squadron name, the radar installation, Kessler attribution and year) is tagged [PLACEHOLDER: ...] so the craft is real while no unverified fact could reach publication. The physical relationships (kinetic energy scaling with velocity, hypervelocity impact behavior, the measurement inputs to orbit determination, the cascade mechanism) are the register's required real craft and are described as mechanism, but the numbers themselves are placeholdered for verification.

EM-DASH CLEAN: confirmed. No em-dashes or en-dashes used as dashes anywhere; every snap and appositive is carried by a semicolon, a colon, a comma pair, or a sentence break. No emojis.

## Before / After repair exemplar

**Register:** The Ledger
**Purpose:** the same news written first in full AI-tell mode, then repaired into a clean Ledger. Read the WHAT CHANGED list as a checklist of the most common tells and their fixes.
**Topic label:** [PLACEHOLDER: a regional utility raising electricity rates to fund grid upgrades, current week's news].

BEFORE:
What does it really mean for a community to keep the lights on? In today's world, the answer is rarely simple. This week, [PLACEHOLDER: regional utility] announced a [PLACEHOLDER: percentage] rate increase, a pivotal decision that underscores the crucial role infrastructure plays in modern life. The move is not just a price change, it is a testament to the enduring challenge of balancing reliability and affordability. Studies show that aging grids face mounting pressure, and experts agree that investment is overdue. The utility's plan showcases a comprehensive strategy. It will fund [PLACEHOLDER: upgrade]. ### What changed

- Cut the throat-clearing rhetorical-question opener ("What does it really mean...?") and the "In today's world" cosmic frame. Replaced with the concrete fact that anchors the whole piece: a specific dollar figure the utility wants, plus the filing date and the ruling deadline. (Structural tell: rhetorical-question opener; "In today's world" opener.)
- Removed "pivotal," "crucial role," "comprehensive," "harness," "bolster," "showcases," "compelling," "striking." (Lexical Families A, B, F.)
- Killed the significance-inflation verbs and codas: "underscores the crucial role," "a testament to the enduring challenge," "reflects a broader trend," "a powerful reminder that," "stands as." Replaced assertion-of-importance with a demonstrated stake, the outage number and the income/arrears statistic carry the weight instead of a sentence claiming weight exists. (Family C; significance-coda discourse tell; the "show, don't assert stakes" craft move.)
- Replaced copula-avoidance ("plays a vital role," "showcases a strategy") with plain verbs: "wants," "says," "funds," "is." (Family D.)
- Deleted "studies show" and "experts agree." Every claim now routes to a named, checkable source: the utility's filing, the named consumer advocate quoted in a named outlet, the regulator's deadline. This serves the accuracy gate as well as the AI-tell rule. (Discourse tell: vague authority; doubles as accuracy gate.)
- Broke the autopilot tricolons. "Fund X. Harness Y. Bolster Z." and "infrastructure, affordability, and accountability" and "connected, resilient, and ready" were all decorative threes. Removed; the one surviving series ("the grid is old, the last storm proved it, and someone pays either way") is a real, escalating concession, not a rhythm. (Tricolon-on-autopilot syntactic tell; tricolon counterfeit.)
- Converted the false-balance mush ("Both sides made compelling points... Critics argue... Supporters note...") into a genuine concession that resolves: grant the company its case, then turn to the narrower question regulators must answer. Concede-then-advance, not concede-then-balance. (Discourse tell: false balance; concession counterfeit.)
- Cut both filler frames: "At the end of the day" and the "Despite the challenges, the future looks bright" speculative both-handed coda. (Structural tells: filler frame; "Challenges and Future Directions" close.)
- Replaced the tidy three-point bow ("infrastructure, affordability, and accountability must work hand in hand") with a button: two short sentences that narrow the question to one household in one month. The piece lands on the concrete stake, not a homily. (Structural tell: tidy three-point bow; short-declarative craft move at the close.)
- Broke the low-burstiness, subject-first monotone. The AFTER swings from a 40-plus-word accumulating sentence (the "[dollar] a month is [percentage]..." line) to a four-word fragment ("The weakest is the timing.") and a deliberate two-word fragment close ("Whether this household..."). Sentence openings vary off the grammatical subject ("So grant the company its case," "Not whether the grid needs money"). (Syntactic tells: low burstiness; monotone clause architecture.)
- Register hit (The Ledger): the piece keeps an accounting frame throughout, who pays versus who decides, the math "worth following," fronting the cost, and stays neutral-to-pointed rather than boosterish, with the authorial vantage that the burden's distribution, not the grid's age, is the real question.

### Annotation

BANDS HIT (The Ledger register): cool accounting vantage sustained end to end (who pays vs. who decides; "the math is worth following"; "front it"); neutral-to-pointed tone, no boosterism; one clear stance (the distributional question, not the grid's age, is what regulators must answer). CRAFT MOVES: (1) Opener anchors on a concrete number + filing date + deadline instead of a cosmic frame or question. (2) Stakes SHOWN via outage count and arrears statistic, never asserted. (3) Genuine concession that turns ("So grant the company its case... The question is narrower"). (4) Burstiness engineered: a 40+ word periodic sentence set against a 4-word declarative and a closing fragment; sentence openings varied off the subject. (5) Close is a button that narrows to one household/one month, not a recap. TELLS DODGED: rhetorical-question + "In today's world" openers; Families A/B/C/D/F diction; significance-inflation codas; copula-avoidance; "studies show"/"experts agree" (folded into the accuracy gate, every claim now names a checkable source); autopilot tricolons (the surviving three-part series is a real escalating concession); false balance; "At the end of the day" filler; "Challenges and Future Directions" bright-future coda; tidy three-point bow.
