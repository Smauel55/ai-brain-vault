# Caught Up AI - Licensing Verification (D-009: untouched article + separate apparatus)

Output of a 31-agent adversarial verification workflow (2026-06-04) testing whether reproducing articles verbatim with a physically separate rhetorical-device apparatus opens new licensing avenues, at what price for a small budget, and whether the viable mix can supply 3-5 classroom articles/week. # DECISION MEMO: Does the Reproduction-Only Design Open New Sourcing Avenues, and Can It Feed 3-5 Articles/Week on a Near-Zero Budget?

**To:** Founder, Caught Up AI (AP Lang rhetorical-analysis daily product)
**Re:** Acting on the "verbatim article + physically separate apparatus" legal thesis
**Date:** 2026-06-04

**Bottom line up front:** The design is a real but narrow unlock, and its core legal claim is a litigation thesis, not a settled fact. It does NOT make sourcing free. Your viable engine is a small **free permissive pool** (US federal public domain + a couple of CC-BY / CC-BY-ND outlets, several of them still provisional), not paid syndication. On that pool the honest planning floor is **~2 articles/week, with 3 typical**, at $0 cash but a real and recurring **curation-labor cost**. 4-5/week is not yet a promise you can make. **Advertise nothing on cadence until the volume pressure-test (Step 1) returns a measured survivor count.** This memo covers only third-party reproduction; commissioning original work-for-hire content (your memory's current front-runner) is a separate parallel track, out of scope here.

---

## 1) DOES THE NEW METHOD OPEN PREVIOUSLY-UNAVAILABLE AVENUES?

**YES for one category, but on a legal thesis that is well-reasoned and untested, not a fact.**

State the foundation honestly up front: the verdict's core move is that because the article is reproduced bit-for-bit and the rhetorical markup sits in a physically separate, non-interleaved apparatus, the product **should** be treated as a "collection" rather than an "Adapted Material" / derivative work (17 USC 106(2)), so the derivative right **should not** be implicated. This is the **most defensible reading** of the facts, but it is **untested on these exact facts** and is exactly the point Section 4 sends to a lawyer. Everywhere below, "no derivative work" means "no derivative work *if the thesis holds*." It is a basis to proceed-with-counsel, not a green light.

Two rights are undisturbed either way: the **reproduction right (106(1)) and the distribution right remain fully engaged**, and fair use does not rescue full-verbatim commercial copying (Harper & Row posture). The design removes at most one right from the shopping list, and only conditionally.

**Newly usable IF the thesis holds (because only reproduction, not derivative, rights are then needed):**

- **CC-BY-ND sources for verbatim, paywalled, commercial reuse.** This is the headline unlock. BY-ND is not a NonCommercial license; it permits whole-work reproduction and sale. Before this design, ND blocked you (annotation = forbidden adaptation). The cleanest candidate is **Singularity Hub (CC-BY-ND 4.0)**, but see the provisional flag below; its current guidelines could not be audited.
- **"As-written" reprint/syndication licenses become the right license shape.** AP/Reuters/Tribune/NYT/WaPo deals were structurally near-impossible solo because annotation needed bespoke derivative + moral-rights clearance. Now you need a standard verbatim reprint license, a **price-and-terms question, not a rights wall.** (Affordability is Section 2; mostly it fails.)
- **Direct-from-writer (Substack) licensing.** The writer holds non-exclusive rights and can grant verbatim commercial reproduction directly via bespoke contract, so no CC trap applies. "License it as written, no modifications" is exactly what writers most readily grant.

**Already usable (design changes nothing, no derivative right was ever implicated):**

- **US federal public domain** (presidential/congressional/agency/SCOTUS text): $0, no license, fully open.
- **CC-BY / CC0 sources** (Global Voices = CC-BY 3.0): subject to the overlay/neutrality caveats below.

**Remain CLOSED, and why:**

| Source | Why still closed |
|---|---|
| **ProPublica** | CC-BY-**NC**-ND. The **NC** bars commercial use. Design cannot fix NonCommercial. Dead. |
| **NYT (general content)** | ToS = personal/noncommercial only. Dead unless a bespoke paid commercial republishing license is bought (Section 2). |
| **The Conversation** | License (CC-BY-ND) is now the right shape, but a **contract overlay is a second, independent legal gate the apparatus design does nothing to clear**: "can't sell our material separately," "can't systematically republish," and education/exam use routes to a paid "fees apply" quote. Plus a **mandatory 1x1 tracking pixel** as a binding republishing condition (see Section 4). Relying on the bare CC grant while ignoring the overlay is, per the source, **untested and not launch-safe.** Conditional on a written deal, not free. |
| **Knowable Magazine** | CC-BY-ND but the same independent contract overlay binds: "no commercial use," "no wholesale/automatic republishing." Dead on contract regardless of markup discipline (and wrong genre/length). |
| **Aeon** | Essays = paid-only (~$650/essay), non-topical, 2,500-5,000 words. CC Ideas archive is frozen. Fails on currency + length + cost. |
| **CC-BY-SA sources** | ShareAlike would force your paid apparatus to be freely redistributable. Unusable. |

**Contract overlays are a second gate.** For The Conversation and Knowable, a clean CC license is necessary but not sufficient: the publisher's separate contractual terms ("can't sell separately," "no systematic republishing") bind independently of any markup discipline. The apparatus design clears the derivative-work question only; it does nothing for contract. Treating the bare CC license as a workaround around those clauses is not a launch-safe basis.

**Avenues this memo does NOT cover (scoping):**

- **State/local and other-government text.** Federal works are PD by statute (17 USC 105), but **state and municipal copyright varies by state and is NOT automatically public domain** (court opinions and statutes generally are; agency reports and speeches may not be). Possible incremental pillar, but each item needs a per-jurisdiction check, flag, do not assume.
- **CC0 / openly-licensed institutional commentary** (university op-ed syndication, Brookings/Pew-type content, court systems, .edu). Some of this is CC-licensed and could clear the four filters; it has not been audited here. Worth a scoping pass precisely because federal PD alone is carrying two of five base slots.
- **AI-assisted original commissioning / work-for-hire.** Your project memory (2026-06-04) flags commissioned-original content as the **reframed front-runner** (solves rights + authorship + currency + neutrality). It is **out of scope for this reproduction memo but is the parallel track**; do not read this memo as the complete sourcing picture.

**Net:** the unlock is genuine but small and conditional. It converts "structurally impossible" into "shop for a reprint license or use a clean permissive source," adding at most one or two new clean outlets (Singularity Hub provisionally; OpenMind conditionally), while leaving the paid-reprint avenues legally shaped right but economically out of reach.

**No monthly paid figure is published in this memo on purpose.** Every paid number available is quote-based or secondary, and the writer band is inferred from adjacent rates, not observed deals. Any monthly math built on it would be false precision. The figure is **unknown until the Step-3 blind test returns real quotes.**

### Unit economics: the breakeven you actually need

At **$20/mo** per subscriber, after a conservative ~30% payment-processing-plus-overhead haircut, net is roughly **~$14/subscriber/mo**. So **one paid subscriber funds ~$14/mo of content spend.** To fund one writer-piece/week (~4.3 pieces/mo) you need monthly writer-spend / $14 subscribers:

- If a piece costs ~$50: ~4.3 x $50 = ~$215/mo, so **~16 subscribers** fund one writer-piece/week.
- If a piece costs ~$150: ~4.3 x $150 = ~$645/mo, so **~46 subscribers** fund one writer-piece/week.

These brackets are illustrative (they reuse the unverified per-piece band only to size the order of magnitude, not to commit a price). The takeaway is durable regardless of the exact rate: **a paid writer channel needs only tens of subscribers, not hundreds, to self-fund one piece/week**, which is why "later, at scale" is a near-term threshold, not a distant one, once the blind test sets a real per-piece price.

## 3) CAN WE HIT 3 TO 5 ARTICLES PER WEEK?

**Honest answer: the all-free planning floor is ~2/week, with 3 typical in a normal week. 4-5/week is not a dependable promise on free sources. Do not advertise any cadence until a 2-4 week real pull measures the survivor count.**

The binding constraint is neither law nor cash, it is the **four-filter intersection** every piece must clear: (1) current within ~7-30 days, (2) ~350-600 words, (3) genuinely argument-driven (AP Lang rhetoric, not reportage/explainer), (4) classroom-safe + neutral + US-relevant. Each free source is individually thin against all four, and the confirmed-today portion of the pool is just one source (federal PD).

### Why the floor is ~2/week, not 3

The two reliably-present free sources are **federal PD (floor ~1)** and **Global Voices (floor ~1 after filters; the source lands it at "1-3, lean 1")**. Singularity Hub and OpenMind can **each independently be 0 in a given week** (Singularity Hub is provisional; OpenMind is excluded from baseline). Two independent thin sources can and will bottom out in the same week, so you cannot stack their worst cases into a floor. The honest all-free floor is **1 + 1 = ~2/week**, with **3 typical** when the variable sources contribute.

### Recommended weekly MIX

| Slot | Source | Planning yield/wk (after all four filters) | Status | Cash cost |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **US federal public domain** (curated neutral/institutional: SCOTUS, ceremonial/official speeches; avoid hot partisan) | 1-2 (floor ~1) | **Confirmed** | $0 |
| 2 | **Global Voices** (argument-driven ~33% of output; screen US-relevance + balance) | **~1** (lean 1) | **Gated** (contact email pre-launch) | $0 |
| 3 | **Singularity Hub** (flag the techno-optimist lean in the apparatus) | 0-2 | **Provisional** (guidelines 404, re-pull before relying) | $0 |
| 4 | **OpenMind** | excluded from count | **Conditional** (classification email; NC-dead if it fails) | $0 |
| 5 (paid flex) | **Direct-from-writer**, target ~1/wk, low-partisanship explanatory/civics/history/science writers | 1 (once a small roster exists) | **Later, post-blind-test** | per-piece price TBD by blind test |

**Realistic sustainable cadence:**
- **All-free (slots 1-3, slot 4 as upside): floor ~2/week, 3 typical, 4 only in stronger weeks.** Not guaranteed every week.
- **Free + ~1 paid writer/week (add slot 5): pushes toward a steadier 3-4/week**, with 5 only in good weeks, and only after a real writer roster and price exist.

**Monthly cost of the recommended mix:**
- **All-free path:** **$0 cash.** The real cost is **curation labor: realistically ~5-10 hours/week** to run a daily four-filter screen across 4-5 thin sources, source-check rights per piece, and confirm currency. For a solo founder that is the dominant cost and it competes directly with product, sales, and the commissioning track. Calling this "$0" without naming the hours would repeat the overconfidence this memo warns against.
- **Free + ~1 writer/week:** $0 cash **plus an unknown per-piece writer fee** (set by the blind test) **plus the same labor.** No monthly dollar figure is committed until quotes exist.

**The blunt honesty:** the standing finding holds, the pool of *current, argument-driven, classroom-safe* permissive nonfiction is **thin for a daily cadence.** The design makes the few qualifying works cleanly usable (if the thesis holds); **it does not enlarge the pool.** A strict every-school-day (5/week) argument-driven product is **not reliably deliverable on free sources alone.** Commit to a public cadence **only after** the Step-1 pull, and only at the number the measured survivor count actually supports.

## 4) RECOMMENDED ENGINE CONFIGURATION, RESIDUAL RISKS, NEXT STEPS

### Recommended configuration

- **Confirmed base (free, always-on):** US federal public domain. This is the only source clean enough to build on today.
- **Gated free adds (clear the gate, then rely):** Global Voices (after the regular-republisher contact email); Singularity Hub (after a live guidelines re-pull confirms no resale/anti-systematic clause).
- **Conditional free upside (do not count in baseline):** OpenMind, pending the subscription-news-outlet classification email; NC-dead if it fails.
- **Paid flex (later, optional):** ~1 direct-from-writer piece/week to lift toward 3-4, **only after** the blind test sets yes-rate and price. Size spend against the breakeven in Section 2 (tens of subscribers fund one piece/week).
- **Hard-exclude at launch:** ProPublica (NC); NYT/WaPo/WSJ/AP/Reuters/Tribune (cost + friction + terms); The Conversation & Knowable (independent contract overlays the design does not clear); Aeon (currency/length/cost); CCC (rail only, email-excluded). Revisit premium reprints only at subscriber scale.
- **Parallel track (separate memo):** commissioned-original work-for-hire content, per project memory the current front-runner on rights/authorship/currency/neutrality. Not evaluated here.
- **Format discipline (load-bearing, from the verdict):**
  - Article reproduced **bit-for-bit**, zero inline labels, highlights, brackets, or markers. The entire legal safety rests on this.
  - Apparatus in a **separate column/panel**; anchor devices by quoting the phrase + citing location ("para 3, sentence 2"), never by embedding a marker.
  - Apparatus quotes **short phrases only**, never a near-complete restatement (avoids abridgment = derivative).
  - **HTML email** so CC attribution renders. Note: for any Conversation content, the mandatory **1x1 tracking pixel is a binding contractual republishing condition** (below), not a format preference.
  - **Full CC attribution per article**; strip all third-party images, agency seals/logos; no DRM/copy-lock on CC text.

### Residual risks

1. **Markup discipline (highest-impact execution risk).** If a label ever creeps into the article text, the product becomes a derivative work and breaches every ND source. Enforce separation in the template, not by hand.
2. **The collection-vs-annotated-edition thesis is untested (the core legal risk).** The whole Section 1 unlock rests on a reading that is well-grounded but not adjudicated on these facts. An apparatus that only works glued to one specific article invites a form-over-substance challenge. Physical separation is strongly suggestive but **not dispositive.** This is the lawyer point below.
3. **Contract overlays are a separate, independent legal gate.** For The Conversation/Knowable (and potentially others), contractual "can't sell separately / no systematic republishing" clauses bind **regardless of markup discipline.** The "rely on the bare CC grant, ignore the overlay" workaround is, per the source, **untested and not launch-safe.** Do not treat any CC license as clearing contract terms.
4. **Tracking-pixel / student-privacy exposure.** The Conversation's mandatory 1x1 pixel is a binding republishing condition with **student-privacy and email-deliverability implications** for a classroom product (third-party tracking on minors' email opens; possible spam-filter and school-IT consequences). This is a reason The Conversation stays excluded, not a formatting footnote.
5. **License-variant error (most common fatal mistake).** Per piece, confirm BY-ND vs **BY-NC-ND** (ProPublica/OpenMind trap) and that each piece is outlet-authored, not a syndicated guest piece under different rights.
6. **Volume is an estimate, not a measurement.** The 2-4/week figures rest on short archive samples and could come in lower. Step 1 exists to replace the estimate with a count.
7. **Provisional/conditional sources could collapse.** Singularity Hub's guidelines are unaudited (404); OpenMind's classification is unresolved; Global Voices requires contact-first. If all three fall, the engine is federal PD + a paid channel, re-plan cadence accordingly.
8. **Neutrality/reprisal (product risk, not legal).** The 2025-26 climate means partisan-reading content risks teacher reprisal. Curate federal text toward institutional/ceremonial; flag every source's lean (GV "Left," Singularity Hub techno-optimist) in the apparatus.
9. **Curation labor is the real recurring cost** (~5-10 hrs/wk) and competes with everything else a solo founder must do; under-resourcing it caps real cadence below the theoretical mix.

### Immediate next steps (in order)

1. **Pressure-test volume FIRST (this week).** Pull 2-4 weeks of real candidates across the free pool; count how many clear all four filters. **No public cadence claim until this returns.** The measured survivor count, not this memo's estimate, sets whether you promise 2, 3, or more.
2. **Clear the three free-pool gates (pre-launch, not post-launch):** (a) Global Voices, confirm a paid daily classroom product on CC-BY + their attribution line is fine, and complete the "regular republisher" contact; (b) Singularity Hub, re-pull current republishing guidelines, confirm no resale/anti-systematic clause; (c) OpenMind, ask whether a paid daily subscription email is the "subscription news outlet" (free) bucket vs "commercial product" (case-by-case).
3. **Blind-test the writer channel (sets the only real paid number).** Cold-email 10-15 short-form, low-partisanship, classroom-safe Substack writers; measure yes-rate and per-piece price for a non-exclusive verbatim reprint. Verify each holds clean rights to that specific piece (no prior exclusive/first-rights grab). Only after this do any paid monthly figures become real.
4. **Scope the unaudited pillars (one pass each):** whether state/local government text and CC0/openly-licensed institutional sources (university syndication, Brookings/Pew-type, court systems) clear the four filters, since federal PD alone is currently carrying two base slots.
5. **Build the apparatus template** with enforced physical separation before any content ships.

### Verify with a lawyer (non-negotiable launch gate)

Before launch, get a **short written copyright-attorney opinion** on the single litigable point the whole design rests on: that a verbatim article tightly keyed to a separate apparatus and sold as one inseparable product is a **collection, not a de facto annotated edition / repackaging** (no derivative work under 17 USC 106(2)). The conclusion is well-reasoned but **untested on these exact facts**, Section 1 should be read throughout as conditional on this opinion. Also have the attorney (a) review the **reusable one-page direct-from-writer reproduction license** if you pursue the paid channel, and (b) confirm that relying on a bare CC-BY-ND grant does **not** override publisher contract overlays (The Conversation/Knowable), since that workaround is untested. This sign-off is the single hard gate; do not launch without it.
