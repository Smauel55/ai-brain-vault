Our Heritage

One distillery.
Six generations.

In 1843, Alasdair Dunmore lit the fires beneath two squat copper stills on a bend of the Spey, a mile downstream of the village his father was born in. Six generations later, those same stills still run.

We have refused, in every decade since, to grow beyond what one family can attend to by hand. We make less whisky than almost any distillery of our age. We do so deliberately. The cask we lay down today will be opened by a grandchild who is not yet born.

This is not a strategy. It is the only way we know.

Iona Dunmore Sixth-generation distiller
The Spirit

Four things, done without compromise.

i.

Spring Water

Drawn from the Allt Beithe, a peat-filtered burn that has run black and cold beside the distillery for as long as anyone has counted.

ii.

Slow Distillation

Five squat copper stills, kept deliberately small. The slow run gives the spirit time to gather depth and a quiet, oily weight.

iii.

First-Fill Oak

Bourbon casks from a single Kentucky cooperage we have worked with for forty years. Then sherry. Never anything new, never anything rushed.

iv.

Time

The youngest cask in our warehouse was filled in 2008. The oldest, in 1976. We open none of them on a schedule. We open them when the spirit says it is ready.
